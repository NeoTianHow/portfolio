# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters evaluating Neo Tian How for software engineering opportunities. They are usually scanning quickly, comparing credibility across candidates, and looking for clear evidence of engineering judgment, shipped systems, and practical technical range.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio presents Neo as a precise, credible, technically grounded software engineer. It should help recruiters understand his fit quickly, then give them enough project and experience detail to justify a follow-up conversation.

## Brand Personality

Precise, credible, technical. The voice should feel direct and professional without becoming cold. The portfolio should read like a confident engineering dossier: compact, evidence-led, and easy to trust.

## Anti-references

Avoid looking like a generic SaaS landing page. Avoid looking like an academic resume page. The portfolio should not feel template-driven, over-marketed, or like a static CV copied into a website.

## Design Principles

- Lead with recruiter signal: role, availability, technical strengths, and proof of work should be immediately legible.
- Show evidence before adjectives: project architecture, outcomes, and concrete work history carry more weight than broad claims.
- Keep the technical tone intentional: use technical detail to build confidence, not to bury the hiring signal.
- Make credibility scannable: sections should support quick comparison while still rewarding deeper reading.
- Preserve professional restraint: visual ambition should sharpen Neo's identity, not turn the page into a flashy showcase.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline. Maintain strong text contrast in both dark and light themes, preserve keyboard navigation, support reduced-motion preferences, and keep layouts readable on mobile and desktop screens.
